I work with Professors Eugene Vorobeychik and Alvitta Ottley, with a focus on human-computer and human-AI interaction. I am also a member of the Ann W. and Spencer T. Olin-Chancellor’s Fellowship, which offers a competitive award package and additional professional development opportunities.

Research Interests

I research machine learning systems that support human decision-making, with a focus on explainability, visualization, and human-AI interaction. My work explores how interactive visual interfaces, artificial intelligence, and interpretable machine learning methods can improve usability and analytical reasoning in complex domains such as autonomous systems and scientific data analysis.

Selected Projects

Explainable Peak Detection for Chromatography
Designed visual interfaces and explanation techniques for chromatogram review workflows, integrating uncertainty visualization and natural language explanations into analytical chemistry tools.
Investigating Zoom Modalities for Chromatography
Investigated zoom modality techniques for chromatogram review workflows, determining which techniques lead to higher peak detection accuracy and lower cognitive load.
Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Driving
Developed deep reinforcement learning models combining image-based perception with navigation commands for intersection navigation and lane-following tasks.
